Gathering Insights

By analyzing users, websites, and social media, the following findings were obtained in order:

Audiences are mainly located in California and New York.

ACTA’s content lacks appeal for national audiences.

ACTA’s content primarily features general keywords but audiences are searching via specific terms.

Site speed is negatively impacted by visual content.

Audiences prefer organic & undesigned posts that highlight the real members, employees, and grantees.

More Specific, More Informative. Better Content.

Through path exploration, we found that the “Page not found” errors tend to load from attempts to view the “Tawanda muChinyakara” page, possibly due to typos or out-of-date links, which direct users to error pages. This information should prove valuable for conducting an audit and updating links that are no longer active.

To enhance the Alliance for California Traditional Arts (ACTA) visibility and broaden its audience geographically and by interest, customize website content to cater to users from diverse areas. Emphasizing traditional arts and cultural traditions is pivotal for reaching a nationwide audience.

Replace or expand upon general references in homepage copy with specific names that potential users are more likely to anchor their searches around.

“Cultural traditions” can be supplemented with cited examples e.g. “…a range of cultural traditions from Bharatanatyam dance to Kumeyaay Wildcat songs”

Images can be boosted in the following three ways to make the site load faster:

1. Utilize Next-Gen Formats

2. Properly Size Images

3. Efficiently Encode Images

Create a Google Business account so that knowledge panel can be updated with more information.

Emphasize concise content and visuals for increased engagement. Minimize direct audience solicitations in regular posts, reserving them for Stories with webpage links. Boost interaction with art by incorporating more performance reels and short-form videos, expanding reach and leveraging native Instagram tools for audience growth.
